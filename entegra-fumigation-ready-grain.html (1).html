How It Works

Even Treatment, Top To Bottom, Then A Faster Clear-Out

Left to diffuse on its own, phosphine pools near the source and falls short at floor level and the far corners. That is where treatments fail and where resistance builds. An active recirculation system moves the gas through the whole grain mass so every tonne sees a lethal, sustained concentration, then reverses to ventilate the structure for safe re-entry.

Closed-loop recirculation: gas drawn from the headspace, returned through the floor, driven up evenly through the grain, then vented on completion.

  1. Charge

    Fumigant is introduced at controlled points. No probing through the stack, no working at height over open grain.

  2. Recirculate

    An external fan skid draws gas from the headspace and returns it through the in-floor ducts, driving it up uniformly through the grain.

  3. Hold And Monitor

    Floor and surface probes track concentration. The system cycles the fans to hold the target dose across the full exposure window.

  4. Vent

    On completion the system switches to purge mode, ventilating the structure and bringing it to safe re-entry far quicker than passive airing.

The Method, Explained Plainly

Recirculation Fumigation Is Established Industry Practice

If you have been told that a recirculating, vented grain facility is a one-of-a-kind offering, it is worth knowing the background. Closed-loop and low-flow recirculation fumigation has been researched, published and used in Australia and abroad for decades.

CSIRO SIROFLO And SIROCIRC

Australian-developed recirculation fumigation systems, proven in the field to distribute phosphine evenly through large horizontal storages, including structures that are capped rather than fully gas-tight.

Closed-Loop Fumigation

A publicly documented design in which a fan draws gas from the headspace and returns it through the floor, cycling it up through the grain until concentration is uniform, then purging on completion.

Aeration You Already Have

Perforated in-floor aeration ducting is standard in modern grain storage. Putting it to work for recirculation as well is sound engineering, not a novelty.

The takeaway for growers: the capability to fumigate evenly and vent quickly is available from more than one builder. What differs is how well the facility is engineered around your operation, and who stands behind it.
